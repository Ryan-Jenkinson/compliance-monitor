The EPR deadline cluster (SF 3 June 30, SB 543 July 1, Maine July 1) tells me EPR just got real and is 99 days out.

The impact scores 6.6 and 6.5 confirm these are not noise.
  • The 1–10 per-article company impact score concept is the right architecture. Scoring relative to our business rather than general industry importance is exactly what separates a compliance tool from a news aggregator.

Questions Raised But Not Answered

  • The REACH SVHC score of 7.3 for n-Hexane addition — does that score reflect that we actually USE n-Hexane, or is it a generic industry score? I cannot act on a 7.3 without knowing whether this substance touches our supply chain.
  • Eight new EPR deadlines dropped overnight. Which of those 8 states' programs cover our product category — windows and doors — specifically? Oregon, Maine, New Jersey, Colorado (HB 22-1355) all have different scope definitions. Are we a 'producer' under each law or not?
  • TSCA shows 7 developments with 0 HIGH but impact scores up to 4.7. Why is a 4.7 not HIGH? What is the urgency threshold and is it calibrated to our company or industry-generic?
  • Prop65, ConflictMinerals, and ForcedLabor all show zero developments. Is that because nothing happened in this cycle, or because the pipeline failed to ingest sources for those topics? Those are not slow-moving topics — zero Prop65 activity in a cycle is suspicious.
  • The 182 total articles processed produced only 21 flagged items (7 HIGH, 7 MEDIUM, 7 LOW). What happened to the other 161 articles? Were they scored below a threshold? What threshold? I need to know the filter logic before I trust that nothing important was dropped.
  • Legislative intelligence shows 0 bills tracked. Eight new deadlines were just added, several tied to specific bill numbers (SF 3, SB 543, SB 54, HB 22-1355). Those ARE bills. Why is the bills-tracked count zero while bill-specific deadlines are being detected?

Missing Data

  • Substance-to-product mapping: For REACH and PFAS hits, I need a column that says 'potentially present in: sealants / glazing compounds / hardware coatings' based on our product lines. Without this, every high score is just a research assignment, not a compliance action.
  • Owner assignment per deadline: The 20 tracked deadlines have no names attached. Who in my org owns the SF 3 PRO operational deadline by June 30? That field must exist or the deadline tracker is a wall calendar, not a management tool.
  • Jurisdiction applicability flag per deadline: Of the 8 new EPR deadlines, which states represent markets where our revenue exceeds the producer threshold? A deadline that does not apply to us is noise.
  • TRI reporting baseline: 'EPA Adds PFAS to TRI Reporting Beginning Reporting Year 2026' is a 6.6 impact score item. Do we currently have any reportable PFAS releases or use thresholds that would trigger TRI filing? Yes or no — that answer determines whether this is a watch item or a project.
  • Regulatory trajectory indicator per topic: PFAS shows simultaneous 'adds to TRI' and 'reduces TRI burden' stories. I need a net direction indicator — is the regulatory envelope tightening or loosening? A simple directional arrow per topic this cycle versus last cycle would help.
  • Cost/effort estimate tier per action: Even a rough S/M/L effort flag per high-impact item would help me sequence what hits legal, what hits operations, and what I can handle internally this week.
  • Enforcement activity data: Impact scores appear to be based on regulatory text, not enforcement posture. A TSCA item with a 4.7 score matters differently if EPA issued a warning letter to a window manufacturer last quarter versus if it has never been enforced in our sector.

Noise Concerns

  • The symmetric urgency split — exactly 7 HIGH, 7 MEDIUM, 7 LOW — is statistically implausible and suggests the urgency tiers are being distributed by quota or bucketing logic rather than genuine severity assessment. Real regulatory cycles are not symmetrical. This destroys credibility in the scoring.
  • TSCA new chemical findings (January, September–December, November–December) are status reports about EPA's internal review pipeline for new chemical notices. Unless we have a specific new chemical under review, these are background regulatory rhythm items that should not be surfacing as 7 developments with a 4.7 top score in a 182-article cycle.
  • EPR showing 1 development with a 2.5 impact score — a PSI webinar — while 8 EPR-related deadlines were added to the tracker in the same cycle is a coherence failure. The article pipeline and the deadline pipeline are clearly not talking to each other, and showing both without reconciliation creates confusion about what EPR's actual urgency level is.
  • Three topics with zero developments (Prop65, ConflictMinerals, ForcedLabor) showing up in the topic cards with empty state messaging is visual padding. Either these topics had articles that were filtered out and I should know why, or the sources failed and I should know that — blank cards are not neutral, they are misleading.
  • The 'maps and data links' panel referenced in the UI description: if these are static links to EPA or ECHA websites, they are a browser bookmarks folder dressed up as a dashboard feature. That screen real estate should be reclaimed.
