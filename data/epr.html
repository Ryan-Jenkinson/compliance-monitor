Cross-State Intelligence

The EPR legislative landscape as of March 2026 shows 187 bills across 28 states in a 'loaded spring' configuration, with 11 states holding large committee inventories (NY: 32, WA: 27, NJ: 14, MA: 13) poised for potential spring advancement, while 7 states already have bills past one chamber led by CA's 4 passed_one bills. Three coordinated campaigns are identifiable — battery stewardship (MD, OR, CA, HI, MN), electronics stewardship (IL, MN, MI, NY, NJ), and mattress/materials EPR replication (NY to HI, VT) — all using model legislation playbooks that could expand 'producer' definitions to capture smart window and door components containing batteries or electronics. For a US windows/doors manufacturer, the immediate compliance priorities are NY (34 bills, June session deadline), CA (4 bills at passed_one, market size), and MN (existing EPR infrastructure accelerating electronics scope expansion), with VT and NJ as high-urgency next movers requiring legislative monitoring and industry coalition engagement before Q2 2026 session deadlines.

Watch List
NY
NY leads the dataset with 34 bills — more than any other state — with 2 already passed one chamber including a mattress EPR bill enacted in January 2026. NY is the primary model legislation source for Northeast states. Any NY enactment immediately elevates risk in NJ, CT, MA, and VT. NY's active session runs through June 2026.
→ Assign a dedicated legislative monitor to NY. Review all 34 tracked bills for language that could apply to building materials, packaging, or electronics. Engage NY window/door industry associations (e.g., AAMA NY chapter) to participate in committee testimony on any bills with broad 'producer' definitions. Prepare a NY-specific compliance memo by May 2026.
CA
CA has 4 bills at passed_one stage — the most advanced legislative pipeline of any state — plus 4 in committee. CA's carpet recycling and battery recycling bills demonstrate willingness to expand EPR to construction-adjacent products. CA's EPR laws often become the national template adopted by other states, and CA's market represents significant revenue exposure.
→ Conduct a product line audit for CA EPR applicability, specifically for carpeting/flooring sold with windows/doors packages, battery-containing smart products, and any single-use plastic packaging in shipments. Register with CalRecycle's producer database proactively. Monitor AB/SB bill numbers for any construction materials EPR language in the 2026 session.
MN
MN passed packaging EPR in 2024 and now has 8 bills in committee including a 03-23 electronics/batteries stewardship bill. MN's existing EPR infrastructure (Producer Responsibility Organization already being established) makes it uniquely positioned to rapidly expand EPR scope to new product categories. MN is also an emerging model for Midwest states including IL, MI, and OH.
→ Engage Minnesota Chamber of Commerce and Association of Millwork Producers on the electronics stewardship bill's 'covered product' definitions. If smart window/door components are captured, file comments during the rulemaking phase before the PRO is finalized. Budget for MN producer registration fees in 2026-2027 fiscal planning.
MD
MD has 2 bills at passed_one stage as of 03-20, both related to lithium-ion battery safety commissions. MD's commission model is a precursor to mandatory EPR — commissions issue recommendations that become bills in the following session. MD's short legislative session (January-April) means any remaining 2026 bills must move within weeks.
→ Track the lithium-ion battery commission's membership and mandate language. If the commission is empowered to recommend EPR obligations for battery-containing products, submit manufacturer perspective via public comment. Identify any MD-based distributors or retailers who could be designated as 'producers' under proposed definitions and coordinate industry response.
VT
VT has 7 bills with 1 at passed_one and moved an EPR bill to committee 03-20. Vermont's history as an EPR first-mover (paint, mercury, electronics) and its small legislature mean bills can move from committee to floor vote in 2-3 weeks. VT enactments carry outsized signaling value to other New England states.
→ Monitor VT House and Senate Natural Resources committees weekly through May 2026. Engage Vermont Retail Merchants Association and Vermont Business Roundtable for coalition building. If a building materials or construction products EPR bill surfaces in VT's pipeline, prioritize testimony given the state's fast-moving legislative process.
WA
WA has 28 bills (second only to NY) with 1 advanced and active movement on waste-to-energy and recycling infrastructure. Washington's Department of Ecology has broad rulemaking authority and has previously proposed extended producer responsibility for building materials under its Recycling Enhancement Act framework.
→ Review WA's Recycling Enhancement Act rulemaking calendar for any building materials category additions in 2026. The 'advanced' waste-to-energy bill could affect how vinyl window/door waste is classified — engage WA State Building Council on classification language. Monitor WA's 27 committee bills for any construction products EPR provisions introduced in the short 2026 session (ends March 12).
Next-Mover Predictions
MEDIUM CT CT moved an EPR study bill to committee on 03-20 — study commissions are the precursor step before full EPR legislation. With 1 bill currently tracked and proximity to NY (34 bills, model legislation leader), CT is likely to introduce substantive EPR bills in the 2026-2027 session cycle once the study concludes.
HIGH VT VT has 7 bills (1 at passed_one, 6 in committee) and moved an EPR bill to committee 03-20. Vermont has a history of early adoption on environmental policy (first US state to enact EPR for paint). The combination of active pipeline and progressive policy history makes VT a high-probability near-term mover.
HIGH NJ NJ has 14 bills, all in committee — the largest all-committee concentration in the dataset, suggesting a buildup without release. NJ's governor has supported environmental mandates, and the state often follows NY's lead with a 1-2 session lag. A critical mass of 14 bills indicates imminent floor action.
HIGH MA MA has 13 bills all in committee. Massachusetts has enacted EPR for electronics and paint previously and has a progressive legislature. The 13-bill committee concentration mirrors pre-advancement patterns seen in CA and NY.
HIGH MN MN has 8 committee bills and just moved a circuit boards/batteries/electronics stewardship bill to committee on 03-23. Minnesota has a DFL trifecta and recently enacted packaging EPR in 2024 — the infrastructure and political will are already established.
MEDIUM CO CO has only 1 tracked bill but passed packaging EPR in 2022 (HB22-1355) and has an established Producer Responsibility Organization framework. The low bill count likely understates actual activity; Colorado often moves quickly once a policy template is adopted.
