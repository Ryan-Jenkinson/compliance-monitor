The compliance intelligence dashboard exhibits significant accuracy deficiencies, with 10 of 44 claims (23%) flagged as incorrect and an additional 19 (43%) unverifiable — leaving only 34% of total claims confirmed accurate. Flagged errors are concentrated in PFAS legislative statistics, where nearly every quantitative claim materially misstates the underlying data, and in REACH/TSCA deadline counts, which reflect record-level miscounting rather than substantive deadline errors. Immediate correction of PFAS-related figures and deadline aggregation logic is required before this dashboard can be relied upon for compliance decision-making.

Key Findings

  • CRITICAL — PFAS legislative statistics are systemically unreliable: 7 of 10 flagged claims are PFAS-related, and every quantitative PFAS figure audited is materially wrong. Errors include: '51 states with PFAS activity' (actual: 13 jurisdictions), '16 states at Early Signals stage' (actual: 0 — stage label does not exist in dataset), '19 states In Committee' (actual: ~8 bills in committee stage), '3 states Near Passage' (actual: 5–6 states), '3 states with Bills Introduced' (actual: 10–13 states), '3 states with Rulemaking In Progress' (actual: 1), and '2 states with Enacted legislation' (actual: significantly more). This pattern indicates either a broken data pipeline, a stale or mismatched data source, or flawed aggregation logic applied to the legislative database.
  • REACH deadline count is undercounted: The dashboard reports '2 deadlines across 1 jurisdiction' for REACH, but the database contains 4 deadline records (Article 33 disclosure deadlines for n-hexane and Bisphenol AF each appearing twice). The underlying deadlines themselves are verified correct (March 21, 2026); the error is in the count aggregation, suggesting duplicate records are not being de-duplicated consistently or the count reflects a different grouping logic than expected.
  • TSCA deadline count misrepresents record vs. distinct deadline distinction: The dashboard reports '4 deadlines across 1 jurisdiction' for TSCA; the database contains 8 records collapsing into 2 distinct deadlines (August 31, 2026 and October 31, 2026). This is a record-count vs. unique-deadline-count inconsistency — the reported figure of 4 matches neither the raw record count (8) nor the distinct deadline count (2), suggesting an intermediate aggregation error.
  • EPR last-updated timestamp is misleading: The dashboard states the EPR State Tracker was last updated 2026-03-23, but the database contains records with activity through 2026-04-14 (HB5771, Michigan). The displayed timestamp understates data recency by at least 22 days, which could cause users to discount the currency of the tracker.
  • 19 of 44 claims (43%) are unverifiable due to insufficient data coverage, representing a substantial blind spot in dashboard auditability and an unacceptable baseline for a compliance intelligence tool.
  • Only 15 of 44 claims (34%) are positively verified, with the strongest accuracy in specific deadline dates (REACH and TSCA deadlines verified via database) and narrow SME-confirmed facts. Verified claims are reliable but insufficient in volume to establish overall dashboard trustworthiness.

Recommendations

  • IMMEDIATE: Suspend or clearly disclaim all PFAS legislative statistics on the dashboard pending a full data pipeline audit. Recount all PFAS figures directly against the live legislative database, resolving stage label mismatches (e.g., 'Early Signals / Pre-Discussion' does not exist in current data schema) and correcting jurisdiction counts against actual records.
  • IMMEDIATE: Audit the aggregation logic for deadline counts across all regulatory frameworks (REACH, TSCA, and others). Implement a clear distinction between 'raw database records,' 'de-duplicated records,' and 'distinct deadlines' — and standardize which count the dashboard displays, with labeling to match.
  • HIGH PRIORITY: Update the EPR State Tracker timestamp to reflect the most recent record in the database (2026-04-14). Implement automated last-updated timestamps derived directly from the maximum last_action_date in the source data rather than from manual or pipeline-triggered refresh dates.
  • HIGH PRIORITY: Establish a data freshness and coverage audit process. The 43% unverifiability rate indicates the dashboard is surfacing claims from sources with insufficient grounding data. Introduce confidence or coverage indicators at the claim level so users can distinguish verified from unverified statistics in the UI.
  • STRUCTURAL: Implement a pre-publication validation layer that cross-checks all statistics displayed on the dashboard against live query results at render time, flagging any claim where the displayed value deviates from the live count by more than a defined threshold (e.g., ±10% or ±1 unit for small counts).
  • PROCESS: Require SME sign-off on all quantitative legislative statistics prior to dashboard publication cycles, particularly for rapidly evolving topic areas such as PFAS state legislation. The current SME verification process (2 of 44 claims) is materially under-resourced relative to the error rate observed.
