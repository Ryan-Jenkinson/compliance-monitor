Chemicals

PFASPer- and Polyfluoroalkyl SubstancesA group of thousands of man-made chemicals used in many industries and consumer products. Known as 'forever chemicals' due to persistence in the environment.
PFOAPerfluorooctanoic AcidA synthetic perfluorocarbon that is a PFAS compound. Used in manufacturing of fluoropolymers. Now largely phased out in the US.
PFOSPerfluorooctane SulfonateA PFAS compound formerly used in Scotchgard and firefighting foam. Listed as a persistent organic pollutant under the Stockholm Convention.
PTFEPolytetrafluoroethyleneA synthetic fluoropolymer used extensively as a coating (e.g., Teflon). A PFAS-adjacent compound under scrutiny in some regulatory frameworks.

US Federal

CAAClean Air ActUS federal law regulating air emissions from stationary and mobile sources.
CERCLAComprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability ActAlso known as Superfund. Governs cleanup of contaminated sites.
CWAClean Water ActThe primary federal law governing water pollution in the US.
EPCRAEmergency Planning and Community Right-to-Know ActFederal law requiring facilities to report toxic chemical releases and emergency planning information to EPA and state agencies; relevant for windows/doors manufacturers using or releasing PFAS chemicals.
FIFRAFederal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide ActUS federal statute that governs the distribution, sale, and use of pesticides.
NDAANational Defense Authorization ActFederal legislation that included mandates for EPA action on PFAS, including requirements to add perfluoroalkyl substances to the Toxics Release Inventory.
PPAPollution Prevention ActFederal statute establishing source reduction and pollution prevention programs; works alongside EPCRA to mandate reporting of certain chemicals including newly-listed PFAS under TRI.
RCRAResource Conservation and Recovery ActUS federal law governing the disposal of solid and hazardous waste.
SDWASafe Drinking Water ActThe primary federal law ensuring safe drinking water. EPA sets standards for drinking water quality.
TSCAToxic Substances Control ActUS federal law regulating the introduction of new or already existing chemicals. Administered by the EPA.
TSCA Section 8(a)(7)Toxic Substances Control Act Section 8(a)(7)Federal EPA reporting requirement for PFAS in chemical substances and articles; recent EPA proposal seeks to narrow scope by potentially exempting de minimis amounts and imported articles from reporting obligations.
UFLPAUyghur Forced Labor Prevention ActUS law creating a rebuttable presumption that goods made in Xinjiang involve forced labor, prohibiting their import.

EU Regulation

ESRSEuropean Sustainability Reporting StandardsReporting standards under CSRD specifying how companies should disclose sustainability information.
EUDREU Deforestation RegulationEU regulation prohibiting companies from placing certain commodities/products associated with deforestation on the EU market.
ICSIntegrated Chemical StrategyEU strategy for a mix of chemicals regulations to achieve a toxic-free environment.
REACHRegistration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of ChemicalsEU chemical regulation that manages the risks posed by chemicals. Requires companies to register substances and provide safety data.
SCIPSubstances of Concern In articles as such or in complex objects (Products)ECHA database for articles containing SVHC in concentration above 0.1% w/w.
SVHCSubstance of Very High ConcernSubstances identified under REACH as having serious and often irreversible effects on human health or the environment. Placed on the REACH Candidate List.

EU Directive

CSDDDCorporate Sustainability Due Diligence DirectiveEU directive requiring large companies to conduct due diligence on human rights and environmental impacts in their value chains.
CSRDCorporate Sustainability Reporting DirectiveEU directive requiring large companies to report on sustainability impacts, risks, and opportunities.
RoHSRestriction of Hazardous SubstancesEU directive restricting hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment.
WEEEWaste Electrical and Electronic EquipmentEU directive setting collection, recycling, and recovery targets for electrical goods.

US Agency

CBPCustoms and Border ProtectionUS agency that enforces trade laws including the Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act (UFLPA) through import detention orders.
CPSCConsumer Product Safety CommissionUS independent agency protecting the public from unreasonable risks of injury or death from consumer products.
EPAEnvironmental Protection AgencyUS federal agency responsible for environmental protection and enforcement of environmental laws.
FDAFood and Drug AdministrationUS federal agency responsible for protecting public health through food, drug, and device safety.
OSHAOccupational Safety and Health AdministrationUS federal agency ensuring safe and healthful working conditions.
SECSecurities and Exchange CommissionUS federal agency overseeing securities markets, including Conflict Minerals reporting (Dodd-Frank Section 1502).
USEPAUnited States Environmental Protection AgencySame as EPA — U.S. Environmental Protection Agency.

EU Agency

ECHAEuropean Chemicals AgencyEU agency that manages the technical, scientific, and administrative aspects of REACH and other EU chemical legislation.
RACRisk Assessment CommitteeThe ECHA committee responsible for evaluating and providing opinions on proposed chemical restrictions under EU REACH, including the universal PFAS restriction currently under consideration.

CA Agency

DTSCDepartment of Toxic Substances ControlCalifornia state department responsible for regulating hazardous waste and toxic substances.
OEHHAOffice of Environmental Health Hazard AssessmentCalifornia agency responsible for the scientific and policy functions of the California Environmental Protection Agency, including administering Prop 65.

State Agency

DEQDepartment of Environmental QualityState-level environmental agency (Oregon, Michigan, Idaho, Montana). Enforces state environmental regulations.
NMEDNew Mexico Environment DepartmentThe New Mexico state environmental regulatory agency tasked with implementing and reviewing the state's PFAS Protection Act, including exemptions for fluoropolymer-based products.
PRISMProduct Registry and Inventory System for MinnesotaMinnesota's chemical reporting system requiring manufacturers to register products containing PFAS under Amara's Law; critical compliance requirement for companies selling products in Minnesota.

Legislative

ABAssembly BillA bill introduced in the state Assembly (lower chamber).
HBHouse BillA bill introduced in the state or US House of Representatives.
HFHouse FileA bill introduced in certain state Houses (e.g., Minnesota HF).
SBSenate BillA bill introduced in the state or US Senate. E.g., CA SB 54 (plastic packaging).
SFSenate FileA bill introduced in certain state Senates (e.g., Minnesota SF).

Regulatory Process

ANPRMAdvanced Notice of Proposed RulemakingAn early-stage regulatory notice inviting public comment before a formal proposed rule is published.
CUUCommercially Useful UnitsA reporting metric used in Minnesota's PRISM system for PFAS product reporting, requiring manufacturers to quantify and report commercially distributed units containing PFAS.
CUUsCritical and Useful UsesDesignations under PFAS regulations that identify specific applications where PFAS use is deemed critical or useful, potentially qualifying for exemptions or extended compliance timelines in state reporting programs like Minnesota's PRISM system.
de minimisde minimis quantitiesThreshold concept in EPA's proposed narrowed TSCA Section 8(a)(7) PFAS reporting rule that may exempt very small quantities of PFAS from reporting obligations.
NOFANotice of Funding AvailabilityAnnouncement of grant or funding opportunities from a federal or state agency.
NPRMNotice of Proposed RulemakingA formal regulatory document published in the Federal Register inviting public comment on a proposed rule.

Packaging

EPRExtended Producer ResponsibilityA policy approach where producers are given significant responsibility for the treatment or disposal of post-consumer products.
PCRPost-Consumer Recycled (content)Material recycled from products after use by consumers. Many EPR laws set minimum PCR content requirements.
PFAS-freeFree from Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl SubstancesDescriptor used in packaging regulations (e.g., food contact materials) prohibiting intentional addition of PFAS.
PIRPost-Industrial Recycled (content)Manufacturing waste recycled back into production. Distinct from PCR for regulatory purposes.
SUPSingle-Use PlasticsPlastic items intended to be used only once. Subject to bans and restrictions in many jurisdictions.

EPR

PROProducer Responsibility OrganizationA non-profit or industry body established to implement EPR programs on behalf of producers.

Conflict Minerals

3TGTin, Tantalum, Tungsten, and GoldThe four minerals covered by Dodd-Frank Section 1502 (Conflict Minerals Rule). Required to be reported on SEC Form SD.
CMRTConflict Minerals Reporting TemplateStandardized industry template for reporting conflict minerals due diligence, maintained by the Responsible Minerals Initiative (RMI).
RMAPResponsible Minerals Assurance ProcessAudit program operated by RMI to assess smelter/refiner compliance with responsible sourcing standards.

Water / EPA

MCLMaximum Contaminant LevelThe highest level of a contaminant that is allowed in drinking water. Set by the EPA under the Safe Drinking Water Act.
MCLGMaximum Contaminant Level GoalA non-enforceable public health goal for contaminants in drinking water. Often zero for carcinogens.

Air Quality

HAPHazardous Air PollutantPollutants known or suspected to cause cancer or other serious health effects. Listed under the Clean Air Act.
NAAQSNational Ambient Air Quality StandardsEPA standards for outdoor air quality for six principal pollutants (criteria pollutants).
VOCVolatile Organic CompoundOrganic chemicals with high vapor pressure at room temperature. Regulated under the Clean Air Act for air quality impacts.

Climate

GHGGreenhouse GasGases that trap heat in the atmosphere. Includes CO2, methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases.

Standards

ANSIAmerican National Standards InstituteNon-profit organization overseeing development of voluntary consensus standards for products, services, processes, systems, and personnel in the US.
ASTMAmerican Society for Testing and MaterialsInternational standards organization developing and publishing voluntary consensus technical standards.
IECInternational Electrotechnical CommissionInternational standards organization preparing and publishing international standards for electrical and electronic technologies.
ISOInternational Organization for StandardizationIndependent international organization that develops and publishes international standards.

Industry Body

RMIResponsible Minerals InitiativeOrganization providing tools and resources to companies for conducting responsible mineral sourcing due diligence.

International

AICISAustralian Industrial Chemicals Introduction SchemeAustralia's regulatory scheme for imported and manufactured industrial chemicals that is requiring information submissions on 522 PFAS substances, potentially preceding future use restrictions.
GHSGlobally Harmonized System of Classification and Labelling of ChemicalsInternational system for standardizing chemical hazard communication.
OECDOrganisation for Economic Co-operation and DevelopmentInternational organization providing guidance on responsible business conduct including the Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals.
POPsPersistent Organic PollutantsChemicals that persist in the environment, accumulate in living organisms, and pose risks to human health. Regulated under the Stockholm Convention.

Publication

CFRCode of Federal RegulationsThe codification of rules published by the executive departments and agencies of the federal government.
FRFederal RegisterThe official journal of the federal government of the United States, containing government agency rules, proposed rules, and public notices.
USCUnited States CodeThe codification of general and permanent laws of the United States.

Classification

NAICSNorth American Industry Classification SystemStandard used by federal statistical agencies to classify business establishments.

Compliance

EHSEnvironment, Health and SafetyDiscipline concerned with protecting workers, the public, and the environment. Common abbreviation in manufacturing and compliance contexts.
ESGEnvironmental, Social and GovernanceFramework for assessing a company's business practices and performance on sustainability and ethical issues.

Other

FYFiscal YearAnnual fiscal period used by EPA for regulatory implementation timelines; referenced for mandatory PFAS reporting year compliance deadlines.

CA Law

Prop 65California Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act of 1986California law requiring businesses to provide warnings before knowingly exposing anyone to listed chemicals. Enforced by 60-day notice actions.

Chemical Safety

SDSSafety Data SheetDocument listing hazard information, handling, storage, and emergency procedures for a substance. Required under GHS/REACH.

EPA Program

SNAPSignificant New Alternatives PolicyEPA program that evaluates substitutes for ozone-depleting substances under the Clean Air Act.
SPCCSpill Prevention, Control, and CountermeasureEPA program requiring facilities to develop plans to prevent oil spills into navigable waters.
TRIToxics Release InventoryEPA program tracking release and management of toxic chemicals by US facilities. Requires annual Form R reporting.

Geography

DRCDemocratic Republic of CongoCentral African country; conflict minerals legislation focuses on minerals originating from DRC and adjoining countries.

MN Agency

MPCAMinnesota Pollution Control AgencyMinnesota state agency responsible for environmental protection, including PFAS water standards.
