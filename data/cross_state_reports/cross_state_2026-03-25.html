PFAS
8 on watch2026-03-25

The PFAS legislative landscape as of March 2026 represents a coordinated, multi-front regulatory campaign reaching critical mass: 15 states simultaneously have bills past at least one chamber, with Virginia, Maryland, and Hawaii all in near-enactment posture within a compressed 30-45 day window. The Northeast corridor (NY, NJ, MD, MA, RI, VT, NH) is operating as an effective regulatory bloc with synchronized bill introductions, companion bill strategies, and shared model legislation from networks like Safer States, while the Midwest Great Lakes states (MN, WI, MI, IL) are developing parallel enforcement and remediation frameworks with industry exemption negotiations underway. For a US windows and doors manufacturer, the highest immediate risk is in product treatment coatings, fluoropolymer-based weatherstripping and gaskets, and PFAS-containing sealants or adhesives, all of which fall within the expanding 'intentionally added PFAS in products' regulatory scope advancing simultaneously in the 5-6 most active jurisdictions — compliance preparation should be underway now, not triggered by enactment.

6 on watch2026-03-25

The EPR legislative landscape as of March 2026 is at an inflection point, with 187 bills across 28 states reflecting a coordinated multi-state campaign that has shifted from packaging and electronics into batteries, mattresses, and durable goods — categories directly relevant to windows and doors manufacturers. The highest-risk states for near-term enactment are CA, OR, NY, MD, and MI, each with multiple bills past committee stage, while NJ, MA, and MN represent the next wave with large committee-stage pipelines likely to advance in 2026-2027. For a US windows/doors manufacturer, the immediate compliance preparation priorities are: (1) legal review of MI's consumer protection framing and CA/OR battery bill definitions for embedded-product scope, (2) active lobbying engagement in NY and WA before their sessions close in spring/summer 2026, and (3) developing a stewardship plan framework that can be adapted to multiple state requirements, as the convergence of 7+ states within a single session window makes piecemeal state-by-state responses economically unsustainable.
